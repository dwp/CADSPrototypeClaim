Your State Pension

You're not eligible for a State Pension as you haven't got enough National Insurance contributions and haven't enough years to make this up before you retire.

You have 3 years of contributions on your National Insurance record - you need 10 to qualify for a State Pension.

You only have 5 years of working age before you reach 67, which means you'll have a maximum of 8 years National Insurance by the time you retire.

How you could qualify for a pension

If you've worked and paid national insurance abroad you may be able to use those years to fill gaps in your National Insurance.

Get other retirement income

You may have a private pension, either set up by yourself or by your work. Find pension contact details.
